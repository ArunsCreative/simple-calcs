What is EMI?

EMI stands for Equated Monthly Installment. It is the fixed amount a borrower pays every month to a lender to repay a loan. EMI includes both principal and interest components and remains constant throughout the loan tenure.

How does EMI work?

EMI depends on three factors: loan amount, interest rate, and loan tenure. A higher tenure reduces EMI but increases total interest, while a lower tenure increases EMI but reduces interest paid.

FAQ

Is EMI fixed every month?

Yes, EMI remains fixed unless interest rate changes (floating loans).
